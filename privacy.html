Privacy Policy

ICAO ePassport Inspector  ·  Effective: April 24, 2026  ·  Last updated: April 24, 2026

TL;DR — The App does not collect, store, transmit, or sell any personal data or passport data.
No accounts, no analytics, no tracking SDKs, no servers. All verification runs on your iPhone only.

This Privacy Policy governs your use of the mobile application ICAO ePassport Inspector (the “App”, “we”, “our”) for iOS mobile devices, published by VIATREE BOT.

By downloading, installing, or using the App, you agree to this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

1. No Personal Data Collection

  • No user accounts. The App does not require registration, sign-in, or authentication.
  • No analytics or tracking. No analytics SDKs, advertising SDKs, crash reporting SDKs, or third-party tracking frameworks.
  • No network communication. The App performs all verification entirely on-device and does not make network calls for verification purposes.
  • No data sharing. We do not share, sell, rent, or trade any information with third parties.

2. How Passport Data Is Handled

When you use the App to verify an ICAO 9303 electronic passport:

  • NFC chip data groups (DG1 text, DG2 portrait, DG7 signature where present) are read into the device's volatile memory via iPhone's built-in NFC reader.
  • The embedded ICAO PKD trust chain bundled within the App (hybrid_masterList.pem, containing 584 CSCA certificates) is used locally to verify the Document Security Object (EF.SOD) signatures.
  • Verification results (INTEGRITY VERIFIED / VERIFICATION FAILED) are displayed on-screen only.
  • All passport data is released from memory when you leave the verification screen or close the App.
  • No passport data is persisted to device storage, cached, or transmitted off the device.

4. On-Device Cryptographic Material

The App contains the following trust material bundled within the App binary:

  • ICAO PKD CSCA certificates (public keys published by national passport issuing authorities, 583 certificates from the ICAO Public Key Directory).
  • One developer test CSCA certificate used for internal integration testing.

None of this material is collected from the user. It is static read-only data compiled into the App binary.

5. Children's Privacy

The App is not directed to children under the age of 13. Since the App does not collect any personal information from any user, no data is collected from children.

6. Data Retention

Because the App does not collect or store personal data, there is nothing to retain, export, or delete. You can uninstall the App at any time; no residual data about you exists on our servers (we do not operate any servers for this App).

7. Security

All passport chip communication follows ICAO 9303 Basic Access Control (BAC) and/or PACE secure messaging protocols, including:

  • Mutual authentication with the chip before any data group is read
  • Encrypted secure messaging for all APDU command/response pairs
  • Local signature verification using the embedded trust chain

No data leaves the device, so there is no transmission security risk external to the user's own device.

8. International Users

The App is available globally. Since no personal data is collected, international data transfer provisions (GDPR Article 44, CCPA, etc.) do not apply — there is no data to transfer.

9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect legal, regulatory, or operational changes. Updates will be indicated by the “Last updated” date at the top of this document. Continued use of the App after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
